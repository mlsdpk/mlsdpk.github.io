I have been studying reinforcement learning (RL) for more than one year now and I think it's time to take some notes and revise my knowledge and experience about RL, which I hope, will help not only for me, also for other readers who are interested in this topic. I'm going to start off by introducing the most fundamental concept of RL, which is a Markov Decision Process (MDP). Most of the material is referenced from Chapter 3 of the [Sutton and Barto textbook](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf) and Lecture 2 of [David Silver RL course](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLqYmG7hTraZDM-OYHWgPebj2MfCFzFObQ).
{: .text-justify}

**Markov Decision Process (MDP)** can be informally understood as the problem of learning from interaction to achieve a goal. It provides a mathematical framework for modeling the decision making process and are used in many disciplines. The learner in the process can be referred to be as a *decision maker*. In the field of robotics, the term *robot* is used. In control theory, it usually refers to the decision maker as a *controller*. In reinforcement learning, we usually called the decision maker as an *agent*. I'm going to use the term *agent* throughout out this article but keep in mind that the terms such as decision maker, robot, agent, and controller are interchangeable.
