Phone Thiha Kyaw is currently pursuing a B.E. degree in Mechatronics from Yangon Technological University, Myanmar.

The year 2020 was his final year (6th year) of undergraduate student life, and he interned at the Robotics and Automation Research Laboratory (ROAR), Singapore University of Technology and Design. During his internship, he collaborated with many people and published research articles focusing on topics related to robotics, path planning, and computer vision. His research interests focus on motion planning of autonomous robots and designing artificially intelligent agents based on reinforcement learning.

He usually gives talks, holds workshops, and shares his experience with young and enthusiastic students of Myanmar. He also participated in many different robotic competitions. In 2017, he was chosen to lead the team representing his country Myanmar to participate in the First Global Challenge 2017, a robotic competition held in Washington DC, and his Team Myanmar achieved rank 6th out of 163 teams. He is also a teaching assistant of the Computer Vision and Machine Learning Lab at Yangon Technological University, Myanmar.
